Domain 1: Visibility

Does your organization know where and how AI is being used by staff? Visibility is the foundation of governance — you cannot manage what you cannot see.
Does your organization have a clear picture of where AI tools are currently being used by staff?
Are staff expected to disclose when they use AI tools in their work?
Do you know which AI vendors or platforms have access to your organization's data?
Has leadership discussed how AI is being used across departments in the past six months?

Domain 2: Policy

Are there written policies governing how AI may and may not be used? Policy provides the guardrails that turn good intentions into consistent practice.
Does your organization have a written policy governing staff use of AI tools?
Since your organization does not yet have an AI policy, the remaining policy questions have been skipped. Your report will include recommendations for getting started.
Does the policy address which types of data may or may not be entered into AI tools?
Does the policy cover external-facing AI use, such as communications or constituent interactions?
Is there a defined process for reviewing and updating the AI policy?
Please answer all questions before continuing.

Domain 3: Accountability

Is someone responsible for AI oversight, and are there processes for raising concerns? Accountability ensures that governance is not just aspirational but operational.
Is there a specific person or role responsible for AI oversight in your organization?
Is there a process for staff to raise concerns or report issues related to AI use?
Are there defined consequences or corrective actions for misuse of AI tools?
Please answer all questions before continuing.

Domain 4: Board Readiness

Is the board equipped to govern AI adoption and risk? Board engagement is essential for organizational accountability and strategic alignment.
Has the board received a briefing or education session on AI risks and opportunities relevant to the organization?
Does the board have visibility into how AI is being used operationally?
Has the board discussed its own role in governing AI adoption and risk?
